About RPE

RPE (Rating of Perceived Exertion) for strength training refers to a subjective measure of the intensity or difficulty experienced during a particular strength training exercise or set. It is a way to gauge the level of effort and fatigue during a lift.

The RPE scale commonly used in powerlifting ranges from 1 to 10, with each number on the scale corresponding to a specific level of effort. Here is a breakdown of the RPE scale commonly used in strength training:

  • RPE 6-6.5: Light to moderately light effort, with several repetitions left in the tank.
  • RPE 7: Could perform 3 more repetitions before reaching failure.
  • RPE 7.5: May perform 3 more repetitions before reaching failure.
  • RPE 8: Could perform 2 more repetitions before reaching failure.
  • RPE 8: May perform 2 more repetitions before reaching failure.
  • RPE 9: Could perform 1 more repetitions before reaching failure.
  • RPE 9.5: May perform 1 more repetitions before reaching failure.
  • RPE 10: Maximum effort, where no additional repetitions are possible.
